Featured images don't have to come from a CDN. This article's hero image
is bundled with the theme and served straight from `public/images/`. That
makes it perfect for cover art that ships with the post itself — book
chapters, anniversary specials, theme demos.

The image above lives at `/images/coastline.jpg` and is referenced from
the post's frontmatter:

```yaml
heroImage: /images/coastline.jpg
heroImageAlt: A wave crashing onto a rocky coastline at golden hour
```

## Inline images, too

You can keep mixing remote URLs (Unsplash, your CDN, a GitHub raw URL)
inside the body. Below, an unrelated photo from Unsplash:

![A misty forest at dawn, lit from behind](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1448375240586-882707db888b?w=1400&q=80&auto=format&fit=crop)

That image is loaded lazily — no work is done until you scroll near it.

## Why support both?

- **Local** is great for assets that ship with the source (logos, theme
  demos, hand-edited photos, illustrations).
- **Remote** keeps the repo small and lets you swap a photo without a
  redeploy.

You can also opt out of featured images on a per-post basis by setting
`showFeaturedImage: false` in the frontmatter — the body will still
contain whatever inline images you've placed yourself.
